# DPM 2.4 Private Modeling Rubric

Use this rubric only for backend reasoning. Do not expose variable names, scores, formulas, or rankings to the client.

## Sections

- Hidden variables
- Advantage, target, boundary, differentiation, and content assessment
- Authenticity and sustainability constraints
- Translation rule for client-facing language

## Hidden Variables

| Variable | Meaning | Use in output |
|---|---|---|
| A | True advantages | Translate into “最值得展示的真实优势” |
| F | Soft fit | Translate into target partner and fit logic |
| X | Differentiation | Translate into memory points and positioning |
| D_content | Content landing ability | Translate into photo/copy/material strategy |
| G_hard | Non-negotiable gate | Translate into people to screen out |
| G_soft | Negotiable but negative gate | Translate into “可以沟通但会减分” |
| K_auth | Authenticity constraint | Translate into “不能夸大/需要证据” |
| K_sustain | Sustainability constraint | Translate into “长期能执行的展示方式” |

## Advantage Assessment

Do not trust self-claims alone. For every claimed advantage, look for:

- Specific behavior: what the client actually does.
- Recognition: who noticed or benefited from it.
- Duration: one-time performance or stable pattern.
- Target relevance: whether the desired partner cares.
- Displayability: whether it can appear in photos, bio, tags, or conversation.

High-confidence advantages usually have concrete cases, repeated behavior, or third-party recognition. Low-confidence advantages are abstract labels without examples.

## Target Partner Modeling

Use this priority order:

1. Client’s own ideal partner description and boundaries.
2. Past attraction and past mismatch patterns.
3. Relationship pace, marriage/children/city/family/money expectations.
4. MBTI and zodiac only as auxiliary language for communication and emotional tone.

If target expectations conflict with the client’s own situation, soften the output:

- “这类期待需要在展示面中提前表达清楚。”
- “这里可能会缩小匹配范围，但能减少无效沟通。”
- “如果想扩大选择面，可以把 X 从公开硬条件改成私聊后确认。”

## Boundary Modeling

Treat these as likely hard gates when clearly stated:

- Marriage intention and timeline.
- Children plan.
- City/settlement and long-distance resolution.
- Major family boundary.
- Emotional safety: cold violence, verbal abuse, disappearance, control.
- Serious financial values conflict.

Treat these as soft gates unless the client explicitly calls them non-negotiable:

- Height, hobbies, specific school/job labels, zodiac, MBTI, minor lifestyle preferences.

## Differentiation

Good differentiation is not “better than others”; it is easier to remember and relevant to target partners.

Look for:

- A stable contrast: e.g. rational but warm, career-focused but family-aware.
- A lifestyle proof: visible daily habits, home order, cooking, outdoor routine.
- A relationship proof: conflict repair, consistent care, mature boundaries.
- A market contrast: what similar profiles usually fail to show.

## Content Landing Ability

Assess whether the strategy can actually be executed:

- Does the client have usable photos or scenes?
- Are they willing to show face, work/life scenes, or personal details?
- Can the profile prove claims through concrete details?
- Is the proposed tone sustainable for the client?

If content material is weak, output a low-exposure plan:

- Prioritize clear bio, non-face lifestyle details, values, and private-chat proof.
- Recommend specific photos to take instead of pretending existing material is enough.

## Authenticity and Sustainability

Every recommendation must pass two tests:

1. **Authenticity**: The profile does not promise a trait the client cannot support in chat or offline.
2. **Sustainability**: The client can keep this presentation style for more than one week.

If a catchy idea fails either test, do not use it.

## Translation Rule

Convert private modeling into client language:

- “A 高” -> “这些优势最值得前置展示。”
- “F 弱” -> “你想吸引的人对 X 很敏感，所以展示面需要提前说清 Y。”
- “G_hard 冲突” -> “这类人不建议投入，因为会在现实推进中卡住。”
- “D_content 弱” -> “先不要追求复杂内容，重点把资料页说清楚。”
